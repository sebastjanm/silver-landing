## Key Statistics

### The Williams Group 20-Year Study (3,200+ families)
- **70%** of wealthy families lose their wealth by the 2nd generation
- **90%** lose it by the 3rd generation
- Source: The Williams Group, cited by CFA Institute, Worth, LegacyIQ, Nasdaq

### Causes of Generational Wealth Loss
| Cause | Percentage |
|-------|------------|
| Communication breakdown and lack of trust | 60% |
| Unprepared heirs | 25% |
| Family conflict | 10% |
| Poor financial planning | **3%** |

- **Only 3%** of wealth transfer failures are due to poor financial planning!
- **Only 10%** of wealthy families disclose inheritance details to heirs before transfer
- Source: Williams Group, LegacyIQ

### The Great Wealth Transfer
- **$84-124 trillion** to transfer in coming decades (Cerulli Associates, Credit Suisse)
- Baby Boomers: $53 trillion of the total
- Most families are structurally unprepared
- Americans 70+ control **30%** of U.S. wealth

### Family Business Survival Rates
- 30% survive to 2nd generation
- 12% to 3rd generation
- **Only 3%** to 4th generation
- Source: Family Business Institute

## Precious Metals as Wealth Preservation

### Central Bank Gold Purchases (World Gold Council 2025)
- **863 tonnes** in 2025 (upper end of expected range)
- **1,000+ tonnes** annually in 2022, 2023, 2024
- **95%** of central banks expect gold reserves to increase
- Central bank share of total gold demand: **25%** in 2024 (vs 12% in 2015-19)
- Top purchasers since 2021: Türkiye, India, China (600+ tonnes combined)

### ECB Survey: Why Central Banks Hold Gold
1. Long-term store of value and inflation hedge
2. Performance during times of crisis
3. Effective portfolio diversifier
- Also cited: default risks, geopolitical diversification, political risk
- 1 in 4 emerging market central banks cite sanctions concerns

### Basel III Classification
- Physical gold classified as **Tier 1 asset** (same as cash and government bonds)
- Most stable asset category for bank balance sheets

## Successful Family Wealth Preservation Examples

### Multi-Generational Success Stories
1. **Rockefeller Family** - 7th generation
   - Formal family governance since 1934
   - Family office, family council, structured education

2. **Rothschild Family** - 200+ years, 7 generations
   - Strict rules, education requirements, governance

### Common Success Factors

## Benefits of Physical Precious Metals for Legacy Planning

### Advantages Over Paper Assets
1. **Tangible** - can physically hold, no counterparty risk
2. **No default risk** - unlike stocks, bonds, or bank deposits
3. **Globally recognized** - value worldwide
4. **Easy to divide** - coins/bars in various sizes
5. **Private** - not dependent on financial institutions
6. **Simple to transfer** - bypasses many probate issues
7. **Historical track record** - 5,000+ years as store of value
8. **Inflation hedge** - proven over decades

### Estate Planning Methods
1. **Will** - simplest but goes through probate
2. **Precious Metals Trust** - avoids probate, tax planning, asset control
3. **Self-Directed IRA** - for retirement metals
4. **Gifting during lifetime** - reduces estate size

### Key Considerations
- Get professional appraisal
- Document location and access (codes, keys)
- Secure storage (home safe, bank vault, private vault)
- Insurance
- Educate heirs about handling/selling
