## 1. Key Finding Summary

### Slovenia VAT on Silver
- Standard VAT rate: **22%** (Wikipedia confirms 20%, but Slovenia updated to 22%)
- Gold: **0% VAT** (investment gold exempt under EU Directive 2006/112/EC)
- Silver: Treated as **industrial raw material**, NOT investment metal

### EU Legal Framework
- **EU VAT Directive 2006/112/EC** exempts investment gold (Article 346)
- Gold requirements: 995/1000 purity bars, 900/1000 coins minted after 1800
- **Silver NOT covered** - classified as industrial commodity
- Each EU country applies standard VAT to silver

## 2. Legal Methods to Avoid VAT

### Method 1: Swiss Bonded Warehouse (Brezcarinsko skladišče)
**How it works:**
- Silver purchased and stored in Swiss duty-free warehouse
- No VAT charged at purchase
- VAT only due upon physical delivery/withdrawal
- Swiss VAT: 8.1% (if delivered in Switzerland)

**Providers:**
- **Swiss Gold Safe** (swissgoldsafe.ch) - ZFL and OZL warehouses
- **OrSuisse** (orsuisse.ch) - Storage in Zurich, no minimum
- **BullionVault** (bullionvault.com) - Zurich vault

**Storage Costs (annual):**
- OrSuisse: 0.9% for silver, 0.7% for gold
- BullionVault: 0.48% for silver, 0.12% for gold
- Minimum charges typically ~$50-100/year

**Advantages:**
- 100% ownership maintained
- Full insurance (Lloyd's of London partnerships)
- Can sell directly from warehouse (no VAT ever)
- No physical delivery = no VAT liability

**Disadvantages:**
- No physical possession
- Annual storage fees
- Better for larger amounts (€10,000+)

### Method 2: Pre-Owned Coins (Margin Scheme)
**How it works:**
- Pre-owned/second-hand coins traded under "margin scheme"
- VAT only on dealer's profit margin, NOT full value
- VAT included in price, not shown separately

**Example:**
- Dealer buys coin for €100, sells for €110
- VAT only on €10 margin = €1.67 (at 21%)
- Effective VAT: ~1.5% instead of 21%

**Status after Jan 2025:**
- Margin scheme abolished for NEW coins
- Pre-owned coins still qualify
- Year of minting doesn't matter (2021 = same silver as 2025)

**Sources:**
- The Silver Mountain (NL)
- Germany changed rules in 2025

### Method 3: Elementum Switzerland Storage
**How it works:**
- Slovenian company with Swiss storage
- Buy through savings account
- Silver stored in Swiss bonded warehouse
- "Good delivery" status maintained

**Details from elementum.si:**
- Partner for Slovenians
- Direct consultation available
- Buy-back guaranteed

## 3. What Does NOT Work Anymore

### Estonia VAT-Free (CLOSED June 2022)
- Was: 0% VAT on silver legal tender coins
- Celtic Gold, Liberty Silver operated there
- EU forced closure of loophole
- Now 20% VAT like other EU countries

### German Import Route (CLOSED 2025)
- Was: Lower import VAT on coins
- Could sell under margin scheme EU-wide
- Germany raised rates under EU pressure
- No longer viable

## 4. Slovenian Tax Implications

### Capital Gains on Silver
- 25% tax rate for holdings < 5 years
- 20% for 5-10 years
- 15% for 10-15 years
- 10% for 15-20 years
- **0% for 20+ years** (holding period benefit!)

**Source:** PWC Slovenia, KPMG Slovenia

### Cash Limits (AML Law)
- Cash transactions limited to **€5,000**
- Larger purchases require bank transfer
- ID verification required
