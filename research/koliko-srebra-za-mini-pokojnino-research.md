- **Average gross old-age pension:** €1,077.55/month

- Most common: €600-700 (120,105 recipients)
- Second most common: €700-800 (90,000+ recipients)
- Only 3,300 receive over €2,500

### Key Statistics
- **Total pensioners:** 652,638 (June 2025)
- **Active workers:** 992,755
- **Worker-to-pensioner ratio:** 1.52:1
- **Pension as % of average wage:** 64.4%
- **Average pension duration:** 24 years (women), 18 years (men)

### Special Pensions

- Minimum pension: €352.58

## 4. Retirement Income Strategies

### The 4% Rule
- Traditional safe withdrawal rate: 4%/year
- Updated (Morningstar 2025): 3.7%
- Adjusts annually for inflation
- Designed for 30-year retirement period

### Silver-Based Income Strategy
Unlike dividend stocks, silver doesn't pay dividends. Income comes from:
1. **Appreciation + periodic selling** (most realistic)
2. **Using as emergency fund replacement**
3. **Hedge against pension erosion from inflation**

### Realistic Calculation Model
- Average silver appreciation: 8% annually (conservative)
- Sell 4-5% annually to generate income
- Principal grows with remaining 3-4% appreciation
- Provides inflation protection banks don't offer

## 5. Calculations for "Mini Pension" Scenarios

### Target: €200/month supplement (€2,400/year)
Using 4% withdrawal rate:
- Required portfolio: €2,400 ÷ 0.04 = €60,000

## 6. Key Messaging Points

### Why Silver for Pension Supplement
1. **Inflation protection:** Bank savings lose value at 3-5%/year
2. **No counterparty risk:** Unlike pensions dependent on ZPIZ solvency
3. **Tax advantages:** Silver coins exempt from DDV in EU
4. **Tangible asset:** You own it directly
5. **Historical stability:** 5,000 years of value preservation

### The Urgency Factor
- 64.4% replacement rate means €400+ monthly gap for average earner
- Pension duration: 24 years average
- System under pressure: 1.52 workers per pensioner
- No guarantee current levels maintained

### Accessibility Angle
- Start with just €50-100/month
- No minimum investment
- Can buy fractional ounces
- Build gradually over 10-20 years
