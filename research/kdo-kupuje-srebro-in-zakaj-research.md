# Research: Kdo kupuje srebro in zakaj

## Key Findings - European/Regional Focus

### 1. Germany - Third Largest Physical Silver Market Globally
- **Silver Institute/Metals Focus**: Germany is the 3rd largest market for retail silver bar & coin investment globally
- Physical investment averaged **48.5 million ounces (Moz) from 2020-2022**
- German physical silver market dominated by **bullion coins (~80% of market)**
- End of favorable tax treatment (end 2022) caused demand to slump by **39 Moz since 2023**
- USA, India, Germany, Australia account for ~80% of worldwide bar & coin market
- Source: Silver Institute, "Key Physical Silver Investment Markets" report (Aug 2024)
  https://silverinstitute.org/physical-silver-investment-increasingly-important-to-global-silver-demand/

### 2. German & Austrian Gold Ownership (proxy for precious metals culture)
- **Steinbeis University / University of St. Gallen research**:
  - Germans own avg **205 grams of gold per person**
  - Austrians own avg **220 grams per person** (highest in study)
  - Swiss own only **95 grams per person**
  - German private households own **9,089 tonnes of gold** (2021 Reisebank/Steinbeis study)
  - Austrian private investors hold **388 tonnes**
  - In Germany: **2.7x more gold in private hands than in the Bundesbank**
- Source: IBIS InGold / Handelszeitung / Steinbeis Research
  https://ibisingold.com/en/article/the-germans-and-austrians-have-surpassed-even-the-swiss-in-gold-ownershi
  https://www.mining.com/web/germans-own-over-9000-tonnes-of-gold/

### 3. BaFin Survey (Germany, 2020)
- 1,000 consumers surveyed by OmniQuest for BaFin
- **4 in 10 Germans** have invested in precious metals or plan to
- **83%** of precious metal investors believe it's a secure investment
- **78%** believe precious metals more secure than bank deposits
- **61%** prefer physical delivery
- **60%** invest up to 10% of assets in precious metals
- **47%** purchase from banks, 53% from other providers
- **63%** who bought from banks: bank advisor recommended the investment
- Source: BaFin Precious Metals Survey (Aug 2020)
  https://www.bafin.de/SharedDocs/Veroeffentlichungen/EN/Fachartikel/2020/fa_bj_2012_Edelmetallumfrage_en.html

### 4. Austrian Mint (Münze Österreich)
- Silver Philharmonic launched 2008, initial mintage ~7.77 million coins
- One of Europe's most traded silver bullion coins
- Austrian Mint is fully owned by Austrian National Bank (OeNB), backed by state
- Source: Wikipedia, BullionStar, Austrian Mint website

### 5. European Central Banks Buying Gold (2024-2025)
- **Poland (NBP)**: Increased reserves to **550 tonnes** (Jan 2026), more than ECB's 506.5t
  - Target: 700 tonnes; Gold share jumped from 16.86% to **28.22%** of reserves
  - Source: Euronews (Jan 20, 2026)
- **Czech National Bank**: Purchased **20.4 tonnes in 2025**, reserves reached record **71.6 tonnes** (5.76% of reserves)
  - Source: Radio Prague International (Jan 8, 2026)
- **Global**: Central banks bought **863 tonnes in 2025** (WGC)
  - **43% of central banks** plan to increase gold holdings (record, up from 29% in 2024)
  - Source: World Gold Council, Gold Demand Trends Full Year 2025

### 6. World Gold Council - Global Investment Demand 2025
- Total gold demand exceeded **5,000 tonnes** for first time (value: $555bn, +45% y/y)
- Bar & coin demand at **12-year high: 1,374 tonnes** (+16% y/y)
- Q4 2025 bar & coin buying: **420 tonnes** (record Q4)
- Gold ETF holdings grew **801 tonnes** (2nd strongest year on record)
- Source: WGC Gold Demand Trends Q4/Full Year 2025
  https://www.gold.org/goldhub/research/gold-demand-trends/gold-demand-trends-full-year-2025

### 7. Silver Market Fundamentals
- **5th consecutive structural deficit** in 2025
- Silver price hit record **$54.48/oz** (Oct 17, 2025), +67% YTD
- Global demand: **1.12 billion ounces** (2025)
- Industrial demand: **665 Moz** (2025)
- ETP holdings up **~18%** (+187 Moz YTD to Nov 2025)
- Source: Silver Institute (Nov 13, 2025)
  https://silverinstitute.org/the-silver-market-is-on-course-for-fifth-successive-structural-market-deficit/

### 8. Why People Buy (from BaFin survey & WGC data)
- **Inflation hedge**: 83% of German investors see PM as "secure" (BaFin)
- **Bank distrust**: 78% believe PM more secure than bank deposits
- **Historical memory**: Germans/Austrians have generational memory of hyperinflation (Weimar, post-WWII)
- **Portfolio diversification**: Safe-haven and diversification motives (WGC)
- **Physical preference**: 61% prefer physical delivery (BaFin)
- **Industrial demand**: Silver unique as both investment AND industrial metal (solar, EVs, electronics)
