## Executive Summary

This article compares €1,000 invested in physical silver in year 2000 versus €1,000 left in a Slovenian/Eurozone bank account. The contrast is dramatic:

| Investment | Initial (2000) | Value Today (2026) | Return |
|------------|----------------|-------------------|--------|
| Silver | €1,000 | ~€10,950 | +995% |
| Bank account | €1,000 | ~€1,295 | +29.5% |
| Bank (inflation-adjusted) | €1,000 | ~€740 | **-26%** |

**Key finding:** The bank depositor LOST 26% of purchasing power over 26 years despite earning interest. The silver investor gained ~10x their money.

## €1,000 Investment Calculation

### Silver Investment
- **Initial investment:** €1,000
- **Silver price (2000):** ~€5.27/oz
- **Ounces purchased:** 1,000 / 5.27 = **~189.75 ounces**
- **Current value:** 189.75 × €59.03 = **~€11,203**
- **Return:** +1,020% (~11x)

### Bank Deposit
- **Initial deposit:** €1,000
- **Average interest rate (Slovenia 2000-2025):** ~1.32%
  - Source: TheGlobalEconomy.com / Bank of Slovenia
  - Range: 0.03% (min, Mar 2022) to 4.65% (max, Oct 2008)
- **Note:** Before Slovenia joined Eurozone (2007), rates were higher
- **Conservative estimate using 1.5% average:**
  - €1,000 × (1.015)^26 = **~€1,474**
- **Realistic estimate using 1.0% average:**
  - €1,000 × (1.01)^26 = **~€1,295**
