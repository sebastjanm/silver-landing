## ‚ö†Ô∏è COUNTERFEIT DETECTION METHODS

### Source: JM Bullion, APMEX, StoneX Bullion

**5 Key Tests for Authenticity:**

1. **Weight Test** (Most Important)
   - 1 troy oz = 31.1035 grams exactly
   - Use precision digital scale (0.01g accuracy)
   - Fake coins often heavier or lighter

2. **Magnet Test**
   - Silver is NOT magnetic
   - If attracted to magnet = definitely fake
   - BUT non-magnetic ‚â† definitely real (zinc is non-magnetic too)

3. **Ping Test**
   - Strike coin lightly
   - Real silver: high-pitched ring (sustained)
   - Fake: dull thud

4. **Ice Test**
   - Place ice cube on silver
   - Silver conducts heat fastest of any metal
   - Real silver melts ice very quickly

5. **Visual Inspection**
   - Check mint marks, fonts, edge details
   - Look for imperfections in design
   - Compare to known genuine specimens

**Professional Methods:**
- Acid test (damages coin - not recommended for investment)
- XRF (X-ray fluorescence) testing
- Electronic conductivity testing
- Specific gravity measurement

## üè¶ ETF vs PHYSICAL COMPARISON

### Sources: US SEC, various financial analysis

**Silver ETF Facts (e.g., SLV - iShares Silver Trust):**
- Only "Authorized Participants" (12 major banks) can redeem for physical
- Minimum redemption: 100,000 shares (~$20 million at current prices)
- Regular investors can NEVER get physical delivery
- Annual expense ratio: ~0.5%
- Counterparty risk: depends on custodian

**Advantages of ETF:**
- High liquidity (trade instantly)
- No storage concerns
- Lower transaction costs
- Easy for trading

**Disadvantages of ETF:**
- No actual silver ownership
- Counterparty risk
- Can suspend redemptions in crisis
- Annual fees erode value
- Not protected in bank failure

**Physical Silver Advantages:**
- True ownership, no counterparty risk
- Protection in systemic crisis
- Privacy (depending on jurisdiction)
- "If you don't hold it, you don't own it"
